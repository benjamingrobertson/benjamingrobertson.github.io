About

Who am I?

Hi, I’m Ben, a freelance web developer, marketing consultant, and writer in Greenville, SC. I’m a naturally curious person and always love to learn because it keeps life interesting.

Where am I from?

I was born in North Carolina but raised all over–including Tokyo, Beijing, Chicagoland, and even New England. I like to think that moving and traveling in my childhood taught me some of the most invaluable lessons in life, but I still went to get a BA in Communications and Media Studies from Wheaton College in Illinois.

Where have I worked?

I’ve worked for major media companies like the Chicago Tribune, Apartments.com, Cars.com and Gannett Company doing marketing consulting and advertising sales. I’ve even started my own publishing venture, Second Nature Journal, as well as a non-profit institute just for fun. Check out my LinkedIn profile if you’d like some more details.

What can I do?

I plan and execute strategic integrated marketing campaigns, including paid search, SEO, social media marketing, email marketing, print, and targeted digital display. My favorite part of this? Proving results by sifting through the data to tell a story. Analytics are key to any marketing campaign and I’d say web analytics is one of my specialties.

On the web development side I work with HTML, CSS, preprocessers like Sass and Less, responsive frameworks, WordPress, Javascript, jQuery, and I’ve even dabbled a little with Ruby on Rails.

What do I do for fun?

In my free time I enjoy biking, hiking, exploring Greenville, learning new things, and hanging out at Greenville’s awesome public library.

Robyn E. Miller

Robyn E. Miller was looking for a website to help launch her new consulting business. It needed to be clean, modern-looking, and embody her mission of “cultivating fruitfulness with student affairs.” We went with a nice green color scheme. to emphasis the fruitfulness and cultivation.

It’s a custom WordPress theme based on the Start Bootstrap HTML/CSS template Agency. The grid system is based on Bootstrap and the theme utilizes a few jQuery plugins. The contact form is built with PHP and validates with jqBootstrapValidation.js.
